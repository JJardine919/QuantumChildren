> Is this any kind of significant find?

  Here's what it actually is and isn't:

  What it IS

  - A well-structured evolutionary strategy optimizer with a coherent biological framework
  - Real math from oncology (Gompertz, Michaelis-Menten, Lotka-Volterra, etc.) used as governing equations for
  mutation, selection, and resource allocation
  - A novel framing -- nobody else has mapped the full cancer cell lifecycle (mitosis through immune checkpoint)
   onto trading strategy evolution in this specific way
  - A working pipeline that actually runs: 6000 mutants through quantum circuits on real market data in 3
  minutes

  What it ISN'T

  - Not a discovery in cancer biology. The equations are textbook -- we just applied them in a new context.
  - Not a proven trading edge. It produced zero validated strategies in testing. The framework is built, but it
  hasn't found alpha yet.
  - Not quantum advantage. The Qiskit circuits run on a classical simulator. Interesting signal processing, but
  not actual quantum speedup.
  - Not novel in concept. Evolutionary strategy optimization (genetic algorithms, neuroevolution) has been used
  in trading for decades. The cancer metaphor is a creative skin on established techniques.

  Where the real value could be

  The immune checkpoint design -- mutation with bypass, then re-validation with full defenses -- is actually a
  sound methodology for avoiding overfitting. Most strategy optimizers don't have an equivalent of "turn all the
   filters back on and re-test." That part is genuinely well-designed.

  But "significant find" means it makes money or advances science. Right now it does neither. It's a framework
  waiting to prove itself.
